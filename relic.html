Phase 2: Your Review

Build Your Professional Game Review Outline

You've learned how professional reviewers work (Training Quest) and proposed your game (Phase 0). Now it's time to build your detailed review outline.

This tool scaffolds you through five core sections plus custom sections. For each, you'll plan your content, visuals, and add your unique perspective. When done, download a formatted outline ready to use as your review blueprint.

Your Game

1Introduction

Remember: Hook the audience. Explain why this game matters. Reference industry context, design philosophy, or cultural significance.

2Gameplay

Remember: Describe specific mechanics, not vaguely. Show how systems create challenge or meaning. Use concrete examples.

3Graphics & Art Direction

Remember: Explain WHY visual choices exist. Connect aesthetics to gameplay and narrative.

4Sound & Audio Design

Submission & Grading

Format

Submit your outline as .doc, .pdf, or .rtf through Canvas. Use this tool to generate, then open the file in Word/Google Docs and format as you like before submitting.

What to Include

  • All five core sections (or more with custom sections)
  • Detailed bullet points and notes in each section
  • Visual/clip planning with placement notes
  • Personal insights showing your unique perspective

Grading Criteria

  • Thoroughness: Detailed, specific analysis with examples
  • Organization: Clear structure that guides your review
  • Visual Planning: Strategic placement of screenshots/clips
  • Creativity: Unique structure or custom sections if relevant
  • Personal Insight: Your unique perspective evident throughout

Canvas is your submission system. Use this tool to structure your outline, then submit your formatted version through Canvas. Your instructor reviews the file you submit, not this tool.
