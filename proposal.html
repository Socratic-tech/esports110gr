PHASE 0 — GAME PROPOSAL

Before beginning your full review or analysis, you must select and justify the game you plan to study. This proposal ensures that your chosen title is appropriate for academic evaluation and provides enough critical material for a meaningful review.

Purpose of the Proposal

The proposal establishes the relevance and feasibility of your project. You must clearly explain why this game is a strong candidate for professional analysis and how you intend to approach your review.

Required Components

1. Justification & Relevance

Explain why this game is worth analyzing. A strong justification may include:

  • Its cultural or industry significance
  • Its distinctive mechanics, aesthetics, or design goals
  • How it represents a specific genre, trend, or issue

Your justification should demonstrate that the game provides substantive material for a professional review.

2. Supporting Context

Provide the essential background a reviewer needs. This may include:

  • Developer, release year, and platform
  • Genre expectations or audience positioning
  • Notable reception history or critical debates

Do not summarize the entire plot or retell the game’s marketing — focus on information that matters for analytical framing.

3. Analysis Preview

Offer a brief glimpse of the areas you expect to analyze. Consider:

  • Key gameplay systems you want to evaluate
  • Aesthetic or audio elements you expect to discuss
  • Any tensions, strengths, or weaknesses you anticipate exploring

This is not a draft — it is a preview of your intended critical approach.

Submission Expectations

Your proposal should be clear, concise, and professionally written. It does not need to be long — it needs to be well-reasoned.

After review, your instructor will respond with:

  • Approved — proceed with Phase 1 and beyond
  • Revision Requested — clarify or strengthen your justification

Important: You may continue training modules while awaiting approval. The proposal does not block your learning progress; it simply ensures your chosen game is suitable for final evaluation.
